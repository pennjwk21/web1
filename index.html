      Conceptually, integrating time into our models is not all that different from the spatial approach. In predicting both house prices and crime, it was hypothesized that the value here was in part, a function of values nearby. Thus far, many many different types of spatial lag features have been engineered to account for the local spatial process. For processes where time is important, a similar hypothesis can be positted - that the value at this time is in part, a function of values at previous time periods. In spatial analysis, spatial autocorrelation is the term used to describe nearby dependence. In time series analysis, the term serial autocorrelation describes dependence in time.
